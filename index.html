Built around comfort

Each EcoCabin balances light, space, and energy use. Timber structures, careful detailing, and high-performance insulation come together in a compact footprint that feels open and calm — a quiet base in nature, without giving up everyday comfort.

Innovation

We aim to keep developing EcoCabins so they move toward being fully sustainable and circular, while staying affordable. With sun, wind, and high-grade insulation working together, you get a home that is easier to heat and pleasant to live in all year.
Every EcoCabin is designed for a high insulation value and built with sustainable and natural materials wherever possible. The rounded corners are a signature detail, made possible by how we use materials — combining performance with a soft, modern look.

Wood

The load-bearing structure of EcoCabins is timber. Wood is renewable, stores carbon, and gives a warm interior character while keeping the build light and adaptable.

Finishing

Interior and exterior finishes are chosen for durability and low maintenance. Materials are combined so details stay crisp and the cabin ages gracefully outdoors.

Steel frame

The steel base is strong and stiff, giving a stable foundation for transport and assembly while keeping the structure reliable on different ground conditions.

Glass wool insulation

Glass wool together with the timber frame helps fill the cavity evenly, reducing thermal bridges and keeping heat inside when it is cold.

Triple glazing

Triple-glazed units improve comfort near large windows: less draft, less noise, and better control of heat loss in winter and overheating in summer.

Infrared floor

The floor can be warmed with gentle radiant heat, ideally paired with power from the cabin’s own solar setup for efficient, even warmth underfoot.
