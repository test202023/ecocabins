Purpose of this website

This site is a small multi-page front-end project for a university assignment. It demonstrates structured HTML, shared styling, and consistent navigation across a fictional brand (“EcoCabins”): home, about, constructions, news, and contact. The content is sample text and images used only to show layout and design — not real products, prices, or services.

Design source

This website was built from the community mockup EcoCabins landingspage on Figma Community.

Who we are

EcoCabins is presented here as a student project brand focused on compact, energy-conscious housing. We describe prefab cabins that use timber, careful insulation, and simple geometry to sit lightly in rural and semi-rural settings.

This page is illustrative only: it does not represent a real company. The text explains the intended idea — comfort, clarity of design, and respect for materials — in plain language.

What we care about

We care about long-term performance: insulation, airtightness, and daylight. The goal is a cabin that feels generous inside without excess square metres, and that can be maintained without constant refurbishment.
We also care about honesty in communication. This website uses generic, educational wording only. It is not a product catalogue, price list, or offer of services.

Our approach

Our approach starts with the shell: structure, insulation, and openings. Finishes and services follow so the space is easy to use day to day. The constructions page shows example types used in this assignment.

If you are reviewing this site for coursework, compare the structure and copy across pages — navigation, hero, and footer stay consistent.
